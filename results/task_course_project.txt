## **1. Impactful Few-Shot Learning Papers (2019+)**

Based on my research, here are 5 impactful papers on few-shot learning and meta-learning from 2019 onwards:

### **1. "Task Agnostic Meta-Learning for Few-Shot Learning" (CVPR 2019)**
- **Authors**: Muhammad Abdullah Jamal, Guo-Jun Qi
- **Summary**: This paper addresses the challenge of task heterogeneity in meta-learning by proposing a task-agnostic approach that can handle diverse few-shot learning tasks without task-specific assumptions.
- **Why it matters**: It provides a more generalizable meta-learning framework that works across different task distributions, making few-shot learning more practical for real-world applications.

### **2. "Graph Prototypical Networks for Few-Shot Learning on Attributed Networks" (CIKM 2020)**
- **Authors**: Kaize Ding, Jundong Li, et al.
- **Summary**: Extends prototypical networks to graph-structured data, enabling few-shot learning on attributed networks by learning node embeddings and prototypes.
- **Why it matters**: Bridges the gap between few-shot learning and graph neural networks, enabling applications in social networks, recommendation systems, and biological networks.

### **3. "Task-Robust Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning" (NeurIPS 2020)**
- **Authors**: Liam Collins, Aryan Mokhtari, et al.
- **Summary**: Improves upon MAML by making it more robust to task distribution shifts through a novel optimization formulation.
- **Why it matters**: Addresses the sensitivity of MAML to task distribution changes, making meta-learning more stable and reliable in practice.

### **4. "Sharp-MAML: Sharpness-Aware Model-Agnostic Meta Learning" (ICML 2022)**
- **Authors**: Momin Abbas, Qian Xiao, et al.
- **Summary**: Incorporates sharpness-aware minimization into MAML to improve generalization and prevent overfitting in few-shot learning scenarios.
- **Why it matters**: Significantly improves the generalization performance of meta-learning algorithms, particularly in low-data regimes.

### **5. "HetMAML: Task-Heterogeneous Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning for Few-Shot Learning Across Modalities" (CIKM 2021)**
- **Authors**: Jie Chen, Aonan Zhang
- **Summary**: Extends MAML to handle heterogeneous tasks across different modalities (e.g., text, images, graphs) in a unified framework.
- **Why it matters**: Enables cross-modal few-shot learning, which is crucial for multimodal AI applications where data may be scarce across different modalities.

## **1. Impactful Few-Shot Learning Papers (2019+)**

Based on my research, here are 5 impactful papers on few-shot learning and meta-learning from 2019 onwards:

### **1. "Task Agnostic Meta-Learning for Few-Shot Learning" (CVPR 2019)**
- **Authors**: Muhammad Abdullah Jamal, Guo-Jun Qi
- **Summary**: This paper addresses the challenge of task heterogeneity in meta-learning by proposing a task-agnostic approach that can handle diverse few-shot learning tasks without task-specific assumptions.
- **Why it matters**: It provides a more generalizable meta-learning framework that works across different task distributions, making few-shot learning more practical for real-world applications.

### **2. "Graph Prototypical Networks for Few-Shot Learning on Attributed Networks" (CIKM 2020)**
- **Authors**: Kaize Ding, Jundong Li, et al.
- **Summary**: Extends prototypical networks to graph-structured data, enabling few-shot learning on attributed networks by learning node embeddings and prototypes.
- **Why it matters**: Bridges the gap between few-shot learning and graph neural networks, enabling applications in social networks, recommendation systems, and biological networks.

### **3. "Task-Robust Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning" (NeurIPS 2020)**
- **Authors**: Liam Collins, Aryan Mokhtari, et al.
- **Summary**: Improves upon MAML by making it more robust to task distribution shifts through a novel optimization formulation.
- **Why it matters**: Addresses the sensitivity of MAML to task distribution changes, making meta-learning more stable and reliable in practice.

### **4. "Sharp-MAML: Sharpness-Aware Model-Agnostic Meta Learning" (ICML 2022)**
- **Authors**: Momin Abbas, Qian Xiao, et al.
- **Summary**: Incorporates sharpness-aware minimization into MAML to improve generalization and prevent overfitting in few-shot learning scenarios.
- **Why it matters**: Significantly improves the generalization performance of meta-learning algorithms, particularly in low-data regimes.

### **5. "HetMAML: Task-Heterogeneous Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning for Few-Shot Learning Across Modalities" (CIKM 2021)**
- **Authors**: Jie Chen, Aonan Zhang
- **Summary**: Extends MAML to handle heterogeneous tasks across different modalities (e.g., text, images, graphs) in a unified framework.
- **Why it matters**: Enables cross-modal few-shot learning, which is crucial for multimodal AI applications where data may be scarce across different modalities.
